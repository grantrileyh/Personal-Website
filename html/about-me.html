In General

I'm a Civil Engineer from Northeastern University. At school I obtained minors in both Computer Science and Mathematics. The classes for my minors were the ones I found myself most passionate about, especially Number Theory, Algorithms and Data, and Object Oriented Design. Now, a recent graduate, I find myself wanting to pursue programming as a career.

Whenever I can, I enjoy backpacking, cooking, and playing music. My passions lie in creating, meeting people, and being present. My hobbies allow me to work hard and improve at something, and have fun while doing so.

Experience

As part of Northeastern's Co-op program I worked 3 full time Engineering positions at firms around the world. These gave me valuable experience meeting non-negotiable deadlines, interacting with clients, and operating in a professional work environment.

I worked as an Environmental Engineer working with water and sewer mains at Environmental Partners in Woburn MA. Working with clients and performing surveying at night prepared me for high-intensity workplaces, and the engineering firm taught me the importance of doing precise and accurate work, and quality control.

For my second co-op I traveled to Singapore to do event management for the Formula 1 Grand Prix that happens in September. At Faithful+Gould, I primarily aided in design and installation of Marquees for vendors and entertainers. This was an incredibly deadline driven high intensity workplace, and I found value and satisfaction in doing a long day of good work.

My final co-op I worked as a Structural Engineer at L.A. Fuess Partners in Boston. Here, several deadlines were concurrently running, leaving employees to work on projects as they saw fit to meet all the deadlines. I learned how to effectively balance and complete several tasks, and collaborate with my coworkers in the smaller office environment.

Skills

For my computer science minor I primarily worked in Java. I feel very comfortable writing code in java, creating tests with existing frameworks like JUnit, using standard Object Oriented design patterns such as MVC or Client/Server, and using extension, inheritance, and composition to create and manipulate classes and data structures.

In my personal development I learned Python, and implemented a couple short scripts and games. The games I liken to a problem set from earlier Computer Science courses, and were built using Pygame to create a GUI.

Finally, I created this website from scratch using HTML/CSS and a little bit of JavaScript. Learning these languages was rewarding and interesting, as this is the most public my work has been. Further, getting into design of the UI, and trying to create a website that follows UX principles allowed me to both learn a lot, and throw myself into a project.

I am currently learning C with the aid of a textbook. I am hoping to gain a further appreciation, and rock solid understanding, of what higher level coding languages are doing under the hood by implementing data structures and past projects in C.
